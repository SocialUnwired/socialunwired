Experience

Background in practice

The following reflects experience from prior roles, included to demonstrate strategic approach and outcomes.

Two environments. Different constraints. The same core challenge — moving audiences from passive consumption to active participation.

National Social Media Campaign · 2025
Canada Federal Election
Digital Communications Manager · March–May 2025

"The substance was already there. The gap was between what the campaign knew and what audiences were ready to recognise as their own story."

Policy-heavy messaging wasn't the problem. Misalignment was. In regions where economic identity pulls voters toward conservative positioning — even when their values don't — traditional persuasion fails because it speaks to the wrong register.

The strategy shifted from policy argument to narrative recognition: low-cognitive, high-recognition formats that met audiences in the tension between their values and their behaviour. The result was not immediate persuasion, but the kind of engagement that builds toward action — debate, participation, and shifting openness to alternative political narratives.

Regional Digital Media Platform · 2023–2025
Alberta Digital News Platform
Digital Communications Strategist · 2023–2025

"In a low-trust media environment, credibility isn't built through information. It's built through recognition — making audiences feel their reality is being reflected, not explained at them."

Alberta's media landscape is consolidated. Centralized narratives fail to reflect local complexity — and audiences shaped by oil and gas dependency, geography, and economic identity don't respond to one-size messaging.

The platform was built as a live testing environment: segmented by economic context and geographic identity, with each narrative frame — healthcare, energy, regional autonomy — tested for resonance across different communities. The result was a platform that built real participation in regional political discourse, not just reach.
